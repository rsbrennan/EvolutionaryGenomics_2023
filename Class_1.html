Study system we’ll be focusing on

Our study system will be the killifish, Fundulus heteroclitus (also called mummichog or mud minnow).

This species is the best for lots of reasons. They’re broadly distributed along the east coast of North America, from Florida into Canada. ADDMAP from fishbase/aquamaps.

They’re the most abundant vertebrate in the habitats where tye live and, given their broad range, they’re super tolerant of lots of conditions: broad temperatures, salinity, hypoxia, among others. They also don’t move much, with an adult typically staying in the marsh where it is born and not moving more than a few hundred meters over its life.

Given the high population sizes and low migration, what might we predict about populations of killifish from different regions of their distribution?
